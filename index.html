Admin User Guide

Everything you need to create, correct, and publish the call schedule. The Admin sub-tabs run roughly left-to-right in the order you use them; this guide follows that flow.

The three tabs

  • Physician View — the read-only calendar the whole team sees. It's the default tab and what a shared link opens to.
  • Admin — all the controls below (PIN-protected).
  • Statistics — fairness dashboard, charts, and the per-physician ledger. The “What do these numbers mean?” panel there defines every column.

1 · Create a schedule

  1. Load the roster & history. Data → “Load report data” loads the physician roster and call history, sets the next 3-month window, and seeds Independence Day. (“…then generate Jun–Aug” loads and builds in one click.)
  2. Set the window. On Setup & Projection, confirm the 3-month window (start/end month & year). Tick “Restrict B call to weekdays only” if you don't staff a daytime B call on weekends.
  3. Review physicians. On Physicians, add/remove doctors or Deactivate anyone not on call this period (deactivating keeps their history). The table shows each doctor's historical calls/month, fairness Δ, and the carry-forward the engine will apply.
  4. Enter time off & holidays. On Unavailability & Holidays, add vacation/CME/conference blocks per physician (they're never scheduled on those dates) and add holidays (or “Add US holidays in window”).
  5. Tune the rules (optional). On Fairness Rules, adjust point weights and limits if needed. Defaults: A=2.4, B=1.0, weekend ×1.5, holiday ×2.0, 1 day rest after a 24-hour call, max 1 consecutive call day.
  6. Generate. On Setup or Schedule, click “Generate projection.” The full window is built and balanced instantly. A green banner confirms success; a warning appears if constraints made some slots impossible to fill.

2 · Read & correct the schedule

On the Schedule sub-tab, use the ◀ ▶ arrows to move between months. Each day shows an A (24-hour) and B (10-hour) call; weekends and holidays are shaded.

Month highlights

The narrative above the calendar auto-explains each month — who's busiest/lightest and why, holiday coverage, time off, and the history correction in effect. It refreshes as you move between months.

Midterm corrections

  1. Reassign one call. Click any A or B cell, pick a different physician, and Save. The picker flags anyone marked unavailable that day.
  2. Lock a call. In that dialog, tick “Lock this assignment” to pin it. Locked cells show a 🔒 and are never moved by a regenerate.
  3. Re-balance around your edits. “Regenerate unlocked” rebuilds the visible month while keeping every locked cell exactly as set.
  4. Rebuild or clear a month. “Generate this month only” regenerates just the month you're viewing; “Clear this month” empties it.
  5. Leave a slot open. Open a cell and choose “Unassign.”

3 · Publish (the rolling monthly cycle)

The schedule is a rolling 3-month window you publish on the 1st of each month.

  1. Roll forward. On Setup (or Schedule), click “Roll forward one month.” The remaining months are frozen so published months never change, the oldest month drops off, and a fresh month is generated and balanced.
  2. Auto-prompt. When the calendar passes the window's start, a banner offers to roll forward to the current month — one click.
  3. Update the shared link. Data → “Download shareable copy” produces an index.html with the current schedule baked in. Upload it to your GitHub Pages repo (replacing the old file); everyone who opens the link then sees the update on any device.

Why this step: the app stores data in each browser, so baking the schedule into the published page is how one schedule reaches the whole team. Re-publishing pushes updates to every device.

4 · How history works

History is a rolling 12-month window of actual calls (it uses fewer months if fewer exist — today it has the 9 imported months, shown on the Setup tab). For each physician it stores monthly A/B/weekend counts.

  • Rate. A physician's per-active-month rate = (their A + B calls in the window) ÷ (months they were actually active). This keeps part-year and new physicians comparable to full-year ones.
  • Δ (deviation). Their rate minus the team's average rate. Positive Δ = historically over-worked; negative Δ = under-worked.
  • It closes the loop. Each roll-forward turns the just-finished month into an actual and folds it into history; months older than 12 are purged automatically, so storage never grows unbounded.

5 · Fairness & the history nudge

Generation is fully deterministic (no AI, no randomness). It assigns calls to minimize a fairness cost while never breaking the hard limits.

What it balances

  • Equal weighted load. Each call is worth points (A=2.4, B=1.0, ×1.5 weekend, ×2.0 holiday); total points are equalized across physicians.
  • Equal A and B per physician. Everyone gets the same number of 24-hour and 10-hour calls.
  • Smooth months. Each physician's monthly count is kept steady (no “1 this month, 5 next”).

The nudge

Each physician's Δ becomes a carry-forward = Δ × correction strength. The engine treats carry-forward as load already done, so historically over-worked doctors get slightly fewer calls this quarter and under-worked doctors slightly more — a gentle correction, not a swing.

Control it with the History correction strength slider on Setup: 0% ignores history (pure forward fairness), 50% is the default gentle nudge, 100% aggressively catches up past imbalance.

Hard limits (never violated)

  • No one is scheduled during their time off.
  • No one works two calls on the same day.
  • At least 1 day of rest after a 24-hour (A) call, and at most 1 consecutive call day (both adjustable on Fairness Rules).

6 · Statistics, security & data

  • Statistics tab — load-variation metric, per-physician ledger, and charts; open “What do these numbers mean?” for definitions.
  • Security sub-tab — turn the Admin PIN on/off and change it. Physician View and Statistics stay open to the team.
  • Data sub-tab — “Download shareable copy” to publish, “Export JSON” to back up, “Import” to restore, “Reset” to start over.

Fairness Rules & Weights Tune how the engine balances load

The engine balances three things at once: (1) total weighted points are equalized across physicians (plus each one's carry-forward), (2) each physician gets an equal number of A and B calls, and (3) weekend & holiday calls are spread evenly. Hard constraints (unavailability, min rest, max consecutive) are never violated. Weighted points = base × day multiplier.

What do these numbers mean?
Hist calls/mo — average A+B calls per active month over the loaded history (Sep 2025–May 2026).
Hist Δ — that rate minus the team average; + = historically over-worked, − = under-worked.
Proj A / Proj B — projected 24-hour (A) and 10-hour (B) calls in the projection window.
A−B — gap between a physician's A and B counts; the engine drives this to 0 (equal A and B).
Proj Wknd / Proj Hol — projected weekend and holiday calls.
Proj load — weighted points: A=2.4, B=1.0, ×1.5 on weekends, ×2.0 on holidays (all tunable under Fairness Rules), plus the physician's carry-forward.
Dev vs fair — % above/below an equal share of total weighted load; near 0% is fair, values beyond ±15% are flagged.
Load variation (CV) — how tightly weighted load is clustered across physicians; lower is fairer (under 8% is tight).
